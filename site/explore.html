Behind the curtain

A fictional company with a real, public warehouse.

Shorelane Commerce is a synthetic B2B2C office-supplies business built as an evaluation fixture for analytics agents. The data is seeded and deterministic, the warehouse is live and public, and the traps are planted on purpose: a plain "what was our revenue?" has five individually-defensible answers, and a naive AI agent picks one with false confidence. Everything here exists to show that human-confirmed context defuses silent SQL — confidently wrong, plausible-looking answers.

Query it right now

The datasets nodal-shorelane.shorelane_raw (landing tables) and nodal-shorelane.shorelane (dbt marts) are shared with all authenticated Google users. Run this from your own GCP project (the free tier covers it many times over):

SELECT measure_name, ROUND(SUM(amount), 2) AS amount
FROM `nodal-shorelane.shorelane.fct_revenue`
WHERE activity_date BETWEEN '2024-01-01' AND '2024-03-31'
GROUP BY measure_name
ORDER BY measure_name;

You'll get five different "revenues" for the same quarter. Which one is the revenue? That depends on who's asking — which is exactly the ambiguity the context layer resolves.

The trap, in one sentence

Net-30 subscriptions are billed up front but recognized ratably, refunds land in a different table than orders, and the marketplace take means gross ≠ net — so gmv, net_revenue, recognized_revenue, billed_revenue, and collected_cash diverge every period, and recognized revenue can exceed GMV.
